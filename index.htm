Last update: 03/14/2014


Yexi Jiang is currently a fourth year Ph.D student in the School of Computing and Information Sciences, Florida International University. His advisor is Professor Tao Li, who leads the Knowledge Discovery Research Group (KDRG) at FIU.

Besides as a student, Yexi Jiang also engages in Open Source as a member of the Apache Software Foundation. He is a PMC member and committer of the BSP-based distributed computing framework - Apache Hama, mainly working on the distributed machine learning module.

Yexi Jiang received his B.E. and M.S. in Sichuan University of China in 2007 and 2010 respectively, under supervision of Prof. Changjie Tang. During the summer of 2011, 2012 and 2013, he was a research intern at IBM T.J. Watson Research Center, supervised by Charles Perng, Anca Sailer and Ignacio Silva-Lepe. During March to July of 2009, he was a full-time intern at Microsoft Research Asia, supervised by Lead Researcher Min Chu.

Yexi Jiang's research interests includes Temporal Data Mining, Distributed Data Mining, Intelligent Cloud, and Semantic Web. You can find more details about him from his Resume.
